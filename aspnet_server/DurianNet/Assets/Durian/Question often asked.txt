Question often asked

Best durian in Malaysia.
Musang King is considered the best durian in Malaysia for its creamy texture and balanced sweet-bitter flavor. Other favorites include D24, Black Thorn, and XO.

How to choose a good durian.
Select a durian with a strong, pleasant aroma, a firm shell with no cracks, and a heavy feel. Gently shake it; seeds should rattle slightly, indicating soft, mature flesh.

Why durian is banned in some places.
Durian’s strong, lingering aroma leads to bans in public places like hotels and transportation, as many people find the smell overwhelming.

Best way to eat durian.
Enjoy durian fresh from the husk for its full flavor and texture. Chilling the flesh gives a firmer, creamier experience. It’s also popular in ice cream, cakes, and pastries.

Durian season in Malaysia.
The main season is from June to August, with a smaller season from November to February. Availability varies by region and weather.

Health benefits of durian.
Durian is rich in fiber, potassium, iron, vitamin C, and antioxidants, which support immunity and overall health. Due to its high calories, consume in moderation.

Most popular durian varieties.
Musang King, D24, Black Thorn, Red Prawn, and XO are among the most popular varieties, each offering unique flavors and textures.

Difference between Thai and Malaysian durian.
Thai durians, like Monthong, are sweeter and less pungent, often eaten underripe for a firmer texture. Malaysian durians, such as Musang King, have a richer, balanced sweet-bitter flavor and are enjoyed fully ripe.
