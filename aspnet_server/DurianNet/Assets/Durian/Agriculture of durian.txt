Agriculture of durian
Best season to plant durian trees.
The best season to plant durian trees is typically at the beginning of the rainy season. This ensures the young trees receive enough water to establish their root systems. In Southeast Asia, this is usually around April to June, depending on the local climate.

How much water does a durian tree need.
Durian trees require consistent moisture but should not be waterlogged. Young trees need around 20-30 liters of water weekly, while mature trees require 40-50 liters, especially during dry periods. Watering should be reduced once fruits start developing to avoid overly watery flesh.

Best soil type for growing durian.
Durian trees thrive in well-draining, loamy soil that is slightly acidic, with a pH between 5.5 and 6.5. The soil should be rich in organic matter and located in areas with good drainage to prevent root rot, as durian trees are sensitive to waterlogged conditions.

How long does it take for a durian tree to bear fruit.
On average, durian trees take about 5 to 8 years to bear fruit if grown from seedlings. Grafted trees, however, may start producing fruit as early as 3 to 5 years. The time to bear fruit can vary based on tree variety, care, and growing conditions.

Recommended durian varieties for cultivation.
Popular durian varieties for commercial cultivation include Musang King (D197), D24, and Black Thorn. These varieties are highly marketable and produce high-quality fruit that is in demand. Other varieties like Monthong and Chanee are also popular in Thailand.

How to plant a durian tree.
To plant a durian tree, first select a sunny location with well-draining soil. Dig a hole about 1-1.5 feet deep and wide enough to accommodate the roots. Place the seedling in the hole, backfill with soil, and water thoroughly. Mulch around the base to retain moisture and suppress weeds.

Fertilizer requirements for durian trees.
Durian trees benefit from a balanced fertilizer with a mix of nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium. Young trees need more nitrogen to promote leaf growth, while mature trees require higher potassium levels to enhance fruit quality. Organic matter like compost is also beneficial.

Common pests and diseases affecting durian trees.
Durian trees are susceptible to pests such as aphids, fruit borers, and mealybugs. Common diseases include Phytophthora (root rot), anthracnose, and leaf blight. Regular inspection, pruning, and proper drainage help prevent infestations and diseases.



Ideal climate for growing durian.
Durian trees require a tropical climate with temperatures between 24-30°C (75-86°F) and high humidity. They need a minimum of 1500-2000 mm of annual rainfall, with no prolonged dry season, making Southeast Asia an ideal region for cultivation.

When to prune a durian tree.
Durian trees should be pruned once a year, preferably after the fruiting season. Pruning helps to maintain the tree’s shape, removes dead or diseased branches, and promotes better sunlight penetration, which encourages healthy growth and higher fruit yield.

How to propagate durian trees.
Durian trees are commonly propagated through seeds or grafting. Grafting is preferred for commercial planting because it produces trees that bear fruit faster and retain the characteristics of the parent variety. Seed propagation is slower and results in variable fruit quality.

How to improve durian fruit yield.
To improve durian yield, ensure consistent watering, use balanced fertilizers, and maintain good soil drainage. Pruning and pest management are also crucial, along with regular mulching to retain soil moisture and provide nutrients. Choosing high-yielding varieties like Musang King can also increase productivity.

Steps to plant a durian tree.
Choose a location: Select a sunny location with well-draining soil that is slightly acidic, with a pH between 5.5 and 6.5. Avoid waterlogged areas.
Prepare the soil: Dig a hole about 1-1.5 feet deep and wide, loosening the soil at the bottom and mixing in organic compost for nutrients. Ensure there is enough space (at least 10-12 meters) between trees.
Plant the seedling: Place the durian seedling in the hole, ensuring it is upright. Backfill with soil and gently firm it around the base to remove air pockets.
Water the seedling: Give the seedling plenty of water immediately after planting. Water regularly, especially during dry periods, to keep the soil consistently moist but not soggy.
Mulch around the base: Apply a layer of organic mulch around the base of the tree to retain moisture, suppress weeds, and add nutrients as the mulch breaks down.
Provide support: Young durian trees may need staking to keep them upright. Use soft ties to secure the tree gently, avoiding any damage to the trunk.
Regular care: Water consistently, apply balanced fertilizers as the tree grows, and protect against pests and diseases. Prune yearly to maintain shape and health.

How much sunlight does a durian tree need.
Durian trees require full sunlight for optimal growth, ideally 6-8 hours of direct sunlight daily. A location with good sunlight exposure promotes healthy growth and better fruit yield.



Can durian trees grow in high altitudes.
Durian trees generally thrive at low altitudes, ideally below 800 meters (2600 feet) above sea level. High altitudes may lead to cooler temperatures and lower humidity, which can impact the tree’s growth and fruiting.

How to improve soil quality for durian cultivation.
Improving soil quality involves adding organic matter like compost or well-rotted manure to increase fertility. Regular mulching helps retain moisture, while maintaining a slightly acidic pH level between 5.5 and 6.5 is ideal. Periodic soil testing can help monitor pH and nutrient levels.

Lifespan of a durian tree.
Durian trees have a long lifespan and can live for 80-100 years or more in favorable conditions. With good care, they continue to produce fruit annually, with peak productivity occurring between 30-50 years.

Does durian tree need pollination.
Yes, durian trees need pollination to bear fruit. Pollination is usually performed by bats, insects, or even hand pollination in commercial farms. Ensuring the presence of natural pollinators or using hand pollination techniques can improve fruit set.

How to prevent root rot in durian trees.
Root rot can be prevented by ensuring proper soil drainage, as durian trees are highly sensitive to waterlogged conditions. Avoid overwatering and regularly inspect soil moisture. Planting in well-draining soil and on elevated mounds can also help.

How to fertilize a young durian tree.
Young durian trees benefit from a balanced fertilizer high in nitrogen to promote leaf and root development. Apply a slow-release fertilizer every 2-3 months and supplement with organic compost or manure. Avoid excessive fertilization, which may damage the roots.

Spacing requirements for durian trees.
Durian trees require ample spacing of at least 10-12 meters (30-40 feet) between trees to ensure they have enough room to grow and receive adequate sunlight. This spacing helps reduce competition for nutrients and allows for easier management.

Average yield of a mature durian tree.
A mature durian tree can yield between 50-100 fruits per season, depending on the variety, age, and growing conditions. Well-maintained trees and high-yield varieties like Musang King can produce even more.

How many years can a durian tree bear fruit?
Durian trees have one or two flowering and fruiting periods per year, although the timing varies depending on the species, cultivars, and localities. A typical durian tree can bear fruit after four or five years.

What are 3 facts about durian fruit?
Known in some regions as the "king of fruits", the durian is distinctive for its large size, strong odour, and thorn-covered rind. The fruit can grow as large as 30 cm (12 in) long and 15 cm (6 in) in diameter, and it typically weighs 1 to 3 kg (2 to 7 lb).
